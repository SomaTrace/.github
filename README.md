# SomaTrace

**Clinical bodywork documentation, built for the treatment room — not the paperwork after it.**

![status](https://img.shields.io/badge/status-pre--launch-orange)
![privacy](https://img.shields.io/badge/privacy-first-brightgreen)

SomaTrace is a tablet-first application for bodywork and allied-health practitioners. During a session, a practitioner marks pain, tension, relief, and treatment context directly on a body map — then reviews how those patterns change across visits and turns them into clinical documentation, without ever breaking the flow of hands-on work.

> **Soma** — the body. **Trace** — to mark, and to record.

---

## Why it exists

Bodywork is deeply physical and deeply longitudinal, yet most of it is still documented from memory, after the fact, in prose. Practitioners either stop mid-session to type notes or reconstruct the whole visit hours later — and the spatial, "where and how much" truth of what their hands found gets flattened into a paragraph and slowly lost.

That loss has a cost. Clients repeat their history every visit. Progress that took months is invisible because nothing captured the trend. The most valuable record a practitioner has — a felt map of a body over time — lives only in their memory.

SomaTrace exists to keep that record where the work actually happens: **on the body, in the moment, and across every session that follows.**

## What it does

- **Capture on a body map.** Mark a finding — where, how much, what kind — in seconds, without leaving the session or looking away from the client.
- **Track over time.** Every session is kept, so a practitioner can compare before and after and watch a client's patterns genuinely change across visits.
- **Draft documentation.** Session events become a reviewable timeline and a real starting point for notes, instead of a blank page at the end of a long day.
- **Keep it private.** Sensitive notes stay protected by design — this is a record built to earn a client's trust, not to mine it.

The product lives or dies on **speed in-session.** A practitioner mid-treatment can't wait on a spinner or a five-field form, so every decision is measured against how fast it is with hands busy.

## Why these founders

SomaTrace is built by two founders who together cover the two things this product cannot fake: **what actually happens in a treatment room, and how to build software worthy of what's recorded there.**

- **The practitioner.** The idea came directly from hands-on work alongside health practitioners — from watching good clinicians lose good information to bad tooling. That founder brings the clinical workflow, the language of the field, and the trust of the practitioner community the product is built for. The features exist because a real practitioner needed them, not because they demo well.

- **The engineer.** The other founder is a systems and security engineer who builds privacy-first software for a living. In a category handling some of the most sensitive information a person has, that discipline is the product, not a feature bolted on later.

Neither half works without the other. Clinical software built without the room feels wrong to the people using it; software built without security discipline shouldn't be trusted with this data at all. SomaTrace is the deliberate pairing of both.

## Status

SomaTrace is in **active pre-launch development.** No real patient data flows through any current build — development runs on synthetic data while the privacy, hosting, and pilot groundwork is completed. This repository is the public home for the project; the application itself is developed privately.

## Disclaimer

SomaTrace is a **documentation and tracking tool for bodywork practitioners.** It is **not a medical device**, it does not diagnose, and it does not provide medical advice. Its body visualizations are a mapping aid, not a clinically validated reference. It is pre-launch software and is not yet intended for use with real patient data.
